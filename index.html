The Holy Quran

The Quran is the holy book of Islam, believed by Muslims to be the literal word of God (Allah), as revealed to the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) over a period of 23 years. It is regarded as the final and most complete revelation from God, following earlier scriptures like the Torah and the Bible.

It provides comprehensive guidance on faith (Iman), worship (Ibadah), and conduct (Akhlaq), addressing theological, moral, social, and legal matters. The Quran emphasizes pure monotheism (Tawhid), the importance of justice (Adl), compassion, and personal spirituality, making it the cornerstone of Islamic life and a source of inspiration for millions worldwide.
